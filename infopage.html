Coping Mechanisms

Coping Mechanisms: These are ways that people manage their stress.
  • Journaling
  • Drawing
  • Reading
  • Singing/playing music
  • Isolation
  • Denial
  • Violent Behavior
Positive Coping Mechanisms: Essentially these would be better than other coping mechanisms because they are healthier ways to deal with stress or anxiety.
  • journaling
  • drawing
  • reading
  • singing/playing music
  • Humor
Negative Coping Mechanisms: These mechanisms will hurt the process of dealing with stress and anxiety.
  • Substance abuse
  • Isolation
  • Denial
  • Hyper Vigilance
  • Violent Behavior
Why healthier coping mechanisms? Healthier coping mechanisms will help bring you to a better state using ways that arent harmful to your physical body

Anxiety Disorder

Ages that are most affected: 14-60 years (more common in adolescence)
  1. Panic Attacks
    • Symptoms:Racing heart, Feeling weak/dizzy, sense of terror breathing difficulty, loss of control, etc.
      Difference from an anxiety attack: Panic attacks are unprovoked and unpredictable.
  2. OCD (Obessive Compulsive Disorder)
    • Symptoms:Obsessive thoughts or compulsions, urges or repetitve behaviors
  3. PTSD (Post Traumatic Stress Disorder)
    • Symptoms: nightmares, unwanted memories, avoiding certain situations, depressed mood.
    • Common Experiences: Emotional detachement, fear, flashbacks, mistrust, loss of interest or pleasure for some activities, insomnia.

ADHD (Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder)

Ages that are most affected: 6-40 years (often begins in early childhood)(males are more affected)
  1. Smoking During Pregnancy
    • When fetuses are exposed to harsh chemicals, they are 2.4x more likely to have ADHD
  2. Television
    • There is proof that the more time spent watching TV shows more attentions problems compared to those who didnt watch as much
  3. Bad Parenting
    • Stresful home environment or parents that dont acknoledge the ADHD make symptoms worse

Depression

More than 100 Million people have it worldwide.
  1. Types of Depression: 6 most common in America.
    • Persistent Depressive Disorder
    • Bipolar/Manic Depressive Disorder
    • Major Depressive Disorder
    • Post Partum Depression
    • Psychotic Depression
    • Seasonal Depression
  2. Symptoms of Depression
    • Extreme irritability over minor things
    • Anxiety and restlessness
    • Trouble with Anger Management
    • Loss of interests in activities
    • Fixation on the past on things that have gone wrong
    • Thoughts of death or suicide
    • Self harm
    1. Physical Symptoms
      • Insomnia or oversleeping
      • Debilitating Fatigue
      • Increased or decreased appetite
      • Weight gain or loss
      • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
      • Unexplained aches
  3. Causes of Depression
    • Low Self Esteem
    • Bullying
    • Anxiety disorder, borderline personality disorder, PTSD
    • Physical/Sexual abuse
    • Chronic diseases(diabetes, cancer, etc.)
    • Alcohol or drug use disorder
    • Genetics
  4. Effects of Depression
    • Headaches, other pains
    • Phobias, anxiety attacks, panic disorders
    • Social isolation
    • Family/Relationship problems
    • Trouble with school or work
    • Attempted suicide
    Facts about Depression
    1. Depressed people dont show that they're depressed physically
    2. Depression is linked to other health problems
